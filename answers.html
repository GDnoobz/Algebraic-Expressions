Question 1


George is at the store. His kids wants cookies. George is on a tight budget, and wants to buy the cheapest cookies that he knows are good. While he was searching, he found 3 different types of cookies that his kids like, all selling for different prices. He wants to buy 10 cookies for his kids. The first cookies were selling for $5 per 2 cookies. The second cookies were selling for $0.30 per cookie. The 3rd cookies were selling for $0.25 per cookie, plus an extra $2.50 for a box to carry them in. Which cookies should Goarge get? Make an algebraic expression to find out.


Answer: The second cookies are the best deal for George. The first cookies are 5c. c = 10/2, c = 5. 5 * 5 = $25. The second cookies is 0.3c. c = 10, 0.3 * 10 = $3. The third cookies are 0.25c + 2.5. c = 10, 0.25 * 10 = 2.5. 2.5 + 2.5 = $5. $3<$5<$25, therefore the second cookies are the best deal.
